In less than three years, PEPFAR’s DATIM program has implemented HIV/AIDS programs in 700,000 facilities across the globe. DATIM, which stands for Data for Accountability, Transparency and Impact, is striving to reach the ambitious 90-90-90 treatment target by 2020 to help end the AIDS epidemic.

With DHIS2 technology at the very core of DATIM, and a tight collaboration between teams based in Washington and Oslo, learn more about how a country program evolved into what is now called the largest health program ever initiated by one country to address a disease.


DATIM: the background details

In 2009 a DHIS2 pilot project was being tested in Rwanda. This project, which was being run by PEPFAR’s Mike Gehron and HISP Oslo’s Lars Helge Overland, was an extension of a Tanzanian health program called Promise. After five years of thoroughly shaping and structuring this pilot project, DHIS2 was deployed successfully and became Rwanda’s first national HMIS. The expertise and experience of this project was then scaled up to an even bigger project; in 2014 DHIS2 became the underlying technology of the largest software system ever dedicated to ending the AIDS epidemic: PEPFAR’s DATIM project.

A data revolution

“Things happened very fast at the time,” recalls Mike Gehron, founder of the project. “Upon the completion of the Rwanda pilot project in November 2014, a new Global AIDS coordinator took up office. Deborah L. Birx had a data revolution mandate that she declared; to roll out a highly granular health system monitored from Washington D.C., to reach 700,000 health facilities across the world - in just four months. As this project was aligned with everything we were hoping to do and already had in place, it was conceivable for us to go ahead with it.”

DATIM’s deployment manager, Mark DeZalia, came onboard and was assigned the role of involving all fifty countries in getting DATIM implementations up and running. HISP Oslo hosted intensive training academies. As the project picked up momentum, teams were formed, the system was set up and in late 2014, DATIM went live across the world.
